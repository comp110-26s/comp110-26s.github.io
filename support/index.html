Support

Where Do I Go For Help?

  • Office Hours: Setup, homework + conceptual questions
  • Email TAs: Logistical concerns
  • Email comp110help@gmail.com: Assignment extension requests, logistical concerns; personal issues and emergencies that impact performance in the course

Office Hours + Tutoring

Office Hours

Come drop in for one-on-one support in office hours with a member of the COMP110 team. With the exeption of Exercise due dates, the average wait time is typically less than two minutes. Please expect to wait longer on Exercise due dates.

Office hours is the ideal place to come for one-on-one help with:

  • Exercises
  • Conceptual Questions
  • Lessons
  • Challenge Questions

Typical office hours are listed to the right, but check the hours on the CSXL website for the most up-to-date availability this week.

Office hour interactions for programming exercises are limited to 15 minutes and one specific question per appointment in order to be as fair as possible to all students enrolled. For longer, conceptual help outside of specific assignments, we encourage you to visit office hours on non-deadline days.

In order to serve every student in the course as best we can, we must enforce a 1-hour wait period between appointments with a given student. During this time, we expect you to continue making substantive progress on your programming exercise. If you anticipate needing significant amounts of help on an assignment, please start as far in advance as possible so you will have multiple opportunities to ask questions as they arise. Realizing you need help more than once in a given day is an indication you would benefit from a stronger conceptual understanding of the material in question and it would be great for you to come work with us during tutoring hours!

Tutoring

Come to Tutoring hours for conceptual help with members of the COMP110 UTA team and small groups of your peers. You can find these locations and links on the CSXL site, just like office hours. If you are feeling a little behind or as though you could use some extra practice, tutoring is for you! Tutoring does not have time constraints (outside of the hours it is open).

Tutoring is a great opportunity to find help with:

  • Conceptual Questions
  • Lecture Review
  • Practice
  • Quiz/Exam Preparation

Note: We must stop taking tickets at the closing time for both office hours and tutoring. If the queue is long enough that we do not believe we will be able to see all tickets before we close, we will turn off the ability to submit new tickets before closing time.

To work with us in office hours or tutoring, visit the CSXL site. Please already be in Fred Brooks/Sitterson Hall when you submit a ticket so a TA can help you as soon as they are available.

Email

In general, we cannot help with programming or assignment questions via e-mail; instead, please come see us in office hours for help on assignments. Over the years, we have learned that it is too difficult, time consuming, and frustrating for you and for us to try and resolve technical issues via screenshots e-mailed back and forth. Thus, we will redirect any such emails to office hours (described above).
